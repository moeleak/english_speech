The History of Game Engines

The 1990s saw the rise of game engines with games like Doom and Quake.
These engines introduced 3D graphics and multiplayer functions.
Game development became more accessible and immersive.

Modern Advancements in Unreal Engine 5

Unreal Engine 5 pushes realism with Nanite (unlimited detail) and Lumen (realistic lighting).
The future of game engines promises even more immersion and innovation.
